Learning From Bad Examples
Intro: In 1977, two Boeing 747 airplanes collided on the runway in Tenerife in the Canary Islands. It remains the deadliest aviation accident in history. Five hundred and eighty-three people died. Investigators later discovered the crash wasn’t caused by mechanical failure. It was caused by miscommunication between the pilots and the control tower, assumptions being made, and a captain who began takeoff without full clearance. After that tragedy, the aviation world did something remarkable. Instead of hiding the failure, they studied it intensely. They studied every decision that led up to the crash. And from that tragedy, the entire airline industry created a new system called Crew Resource Management. It teaches pilots something simple but powerful: Any crew member can challenge a decision / No one is above correction / Communication must always be clear and confirmed. BUT: Because of the lessons learned from that one disaster, aviation today is far safer than it used to be. Thousands of lives have been saved because people were willing to learn from a bad example instead of repeating it.

Story: Talked so much about airplanes today…Did you know that whenever an airplane crashes, investigators immediately begin searching for one thing: the black box. Inside that small device is the cockpit recording and flight data from the final moments of the flight. Investigators will spend months studying those recordings.
