Patience For Your Future Self
Intro: In China, there is type of bamboo called “Chinese Bamboo”. When you plant one of these bamboo seeds, something strange happens: for the first year of caring for this seed nothing happens. Then the second year, & the third. For 5 straight years of watering, fertilizing & caring for this plant, absolutely no visible growth happens. Nothing comes out of the ground. But on year five, all of a sudden, within 6 weeks, this bamboo shoots up over 80 feet tall. Why? Well, this bamboo was growing the entire time, it just wasn’t visible - it was growing a massive root system underground designed to support the sudden growth in those 6 weeks. People who’ve planted these seeds but didn’t understand the process, would give up way too soon - stop watering, stop caring for the seed - not realizing that growth was happening, it just wasn’t visible to them.
- Our own personal growth happens very similar to this - want God’s will / destiny / things life to improve
- But stop pushing for more, because think nothing’s growing: but something is growing: and it’s you
- Talk a little bit this morning about having Patience For Your Future Self…

2. Jesus’ Patience
   a) TXT says something interesting: “may the Lord direct you into…the patience of Christ”
      i) What is the patience of Christ? What does that mean? Why does tell us to use Christ’s patience?
      ii) Christ was perfectly patient in all ways - let’s look at a few:
         1) First (don’t usually consider): Christ knew He was God in flesh - yet waited 30 years before starting His ministry
            a) Have ever thought about this? Why did he wait so long? Me, I would’ve been doing miracles as a kid
Biblical Context: Very interesting: In Old Testament law, a priest was not allowed to begin full ministry until age thirty - they were expected to have life experience, learning, and have something to offer. So if Jesus began before age 30, people at the time would not have respected him as a legitimate teacher. Would’ve written him off as just a crazy kid or something like that. So THINK: We get impatient - why we’re not progressing fast enough in WOG / life. But Jesus had to wait 30 years, knowing He’s God, knowing He could do miracles, He had to have patience until time was right.
         2) Second: Jesus had to be patient with the disciples - disciples didn’t quite get it for a while
In Matthew 15, Jesus says: So Jesus said, ‘Are you also still without understanding? After disciples have been w/ him for years, and still weren’t understanding basic lessons was trying to teach them.
In John 14:9, Jesus is frustrated w/ Phillip: Have I been with you so long, and yet you have not known Me, Philip?
In Mark 9:19, Disciples try healing someone, and doesn’t work: O faithless generation, how long shall I be with you? How long shall I bear with you? Bring him to Me.
            1) We’re like this too, aren’t we? But you know what’s amazing? Through all our mistakes / impatience / failures
               a) Jesus is still patient with us - keeps showing us grace every time we mess up
2 Peter 3:9 The Lord… is longsuffering toward us, not willing that any should perish but that all should come to repentance.
         3) Third: Jesus was ultimately patient while bearing burden of our sin on the cross
Matthew 27:46 And about the ninth hour Jesus cried out with a loud voice, saying, ‘Eli, Eli, lama sabachthani?’ that is, ‘My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?
            a) THINK: Jesus’ had to bear separation from God that he did not deserve
            b) Bible tells us penalty for our sin is separation from God - Christ took that punishment onto Himself
               i) And He had to bear that it in patience
   b) Jesus’ patience was active
      i) Christ’s patience was not just sitting around waiting - He did something in the meantime
Biblical Story: We talked about Jesus having to wait 30 years to start His ministry - but he didn’t just sit around: In Luke chapter 2, we read about Jesus’ parents losing track of Jesus when He’s about 12 years old. They search everywhere for three days, and where do they find Him? At the temple. His says: “did you not know that I must be about my father’s business?”
         1) What does this mean? While remaining patient, Jesus was still doing what’s right - wasn’t just sitting around
            a) Still praying, studying, going about His Father’s business
         2) This is an active patience - “God, don’t know when going change situation, but in mean time, I’ll do right”
Someone said: “Patience is all about finding something to do in the meantime.”
            b) What can do in meantime? Build prayer life / study Bible closer / draw closer to God in times of waiting
Story: A farmer plants his crops in the spring. He puts the seed in the ground, waters the field, fertilizes the soil, and then he waits. But a farmer can’t make the seed grow. He can’t command the seed to sprout. He can’t pull the plant up out of the ground. He can’t force the harvest to come faster. But that doesn’t mean he does nothing. While he waits, he: waters the field / removes weeds / gets barn ready for harvest, because he understands growth is happening even when he can’t see it. If the farmer stopped watering because he didn’t see immediate results, the harvest would never come.
         3) If you stop watering the seed in your soul, the harvest will never come
James 5:7 Therefore be patient, brethren, until the coming of the Lord. See how the farmer waits for the precious fruit of the earth, waiting patiently for it until it receives the early and latter rain.
            a) If a farmer only watered field sometimes do you think he’d have a good harvest? No
            b) He must water consistently - every single day - for the harvest to eventually come
   c) Jesus’ patience was based on God’s love
      i) Jesus was able to remain patient in all circumstances b/c He understood: God loves me

3. Supernatural Patience
   a) TXT tells us that when our patience runs out, we can draw on Jesus’ patience
Story: In 1967, a woman named Kathrine Switzer became the first woman to officially run the Boston Marathon. At that time, women were not allowed to compete. Race officials believed women were physically incapable of running 26 miles. Kathrine entered using only her initials: “K.V. Switzer.” A few miles into the race, one of the race directors realized a woman was running. He became furious, ran onto the course, and tried to physically rip her race number off and drag her out of the race. But the runners around her blocked him, and she kept running. Later she said that around mile 20 she wanted to quit. Her legs were exhausted and she was emotionally shaken. But the people around her kept saying: “Just keep going. One step at a time.” So she did. Step by step… mile by mile… until she crossed the finish line. When she finished, she proved something important: The limits people believed in weren’t real.
         1) You’re not running this race alone - God is running right beside you, saying: “Just keep going - one step..”
Isaiah 41:10 Fear not, for I am with you; Be not dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you, Yes, I will help you, I will uphold you with My righteous right hand.
      i) TRUTH: Human patience eventually runs out: but there’s another kind of patience - supernatural patience fr. God
         1) Paul understood something: if you’re going to make it through life, you cannot rely on your patience 
         2) You must rely on Christ’s patience
VS 5 Now may the Lord direct your hearts into the love of God and into the patience of Christ.
      ii) “I don’t know how I can keep doing this!” / “I don’t know how to stay strong for so long!”

         2) In fact, Bible tells us His strength shows itself when we’re at our weakest
2 Corinthians 12:9 My grace is sufficient for you, for My strength is made perfect in weakness.
         3) At the end of the day - only thing we need to sustain us in journey of life is the Patience of Christ
Closing Ill: In the Middle Ages, when people built great cathedrals in Europe, the construction sometimes took 100 to 200 years. What’s crazy is builders knew they would never see the finished building. A stonemason might spend his entire life carving stones for a wall… knowing he would never see the result of his labor. Yet every day he still showed up to work. He carved the stone carefully. He laid the foundation properly, and built each wall with precision. Why do this? They weren’t building for themselves. They were building for future generations - for others who would come into this building and worship God. 
- Sometimes we get impatient with how slowly we feel like things are happening, but God might be actively doing something that you can’t see. And just like the bamboo stalk, you cannot stop watering your soul, no matter how long it takes, because one day, it’s going to grow 80 feet in six weeks. I encourage you to have patience for your future self. 
- But not just for yourself - just like the builders of these ancient cathedrals, have patience for what God wants you to become to make impact on others / future
