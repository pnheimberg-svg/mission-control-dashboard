Pleasing God
#4 Pleasing God Through Purity
Intro: Been doing Sermon series exploring scriptures in Bible that talk about pleasing God. Today’s sermon is fourth in series, and I want to go back to our original scripture, 1 Thessalonians 4:1 - if keep reading, it goes on to talk about purity. 
- Want to speak for few moments about Pleasing God Through Purity
Story: Have you ever gone to fill up a cup of water from the tap and it comes out brown? Nasty. Imagine you went to someone’s house and they gave that to you: be like: “Uh..I think this water’s dirty…” Imagine they replied “Oh, it’s ok it’s just a little sewage..” No way, that’s nasty - you’d want a different cup of course. Why? Because when something is supposed to be clean, even a little contamination changes everything. 
- Bible uses same idea when talks about purity in the Christian life - going to talk about Pleasing God Through Purity


1 Thessalonians 4:1-8 Finally then, brethren, we urge and exhort in the Lord Jesus that you should abound more and more, just as you received from us how you ought to walk and to please God; for you know what commandments we gave you through the Lord Jesus.
For this is the will of God, your sanctification: that you should abstain from sexual immorality; that each of you should know how to possess his own vessel in sanctification and honor, not in passion of lust, like the Gentiles who do not know God; that no one should take advantage of and defraud his brother in this matter, because the Lord is the avenger of all such, as we also forewarned you and testified. For God did not call us to uncleanness, but in holiness. Therefore he who rejects this does not reject man, but God, who has also given us His Holy Spirit.


1. The Purpose of Purity
   a) Moral purity is absolutely contrary to the spirit of our society
      i) Rise of promiscuity, homosexuality, pornography, every type of impure sin
         1) But this is not just true of today - this was true at time this scripture was written
Historical Context: A writer in Greece about same time said: we keep prostitutes for pleasure; mistresses for the day to day needs of the body; and wives for the begetting of children & the home.
            a) Almost all roman emperors & caesars were homosexuals
         2) They had no shame about this at all: in fact, you would be viewed as strange if you were not promiscuous
   b) This true in our society too:
Someone said: “The most important event in human history is contraception.” 
      i) Why? Because it allowed the so-called “sexual revolution” - what I call: immorality without consequences
Story: (US) 3 major TV networks: prime afternoon/evening hours - broadcast over 65,000 sexual references per year. The average American viewer sees over 14,000 references/depictions of sex per year. One newspaper interviewed women: The question they asked was: “Would you go out w/ a man who had a moral opinion about sex, who wanted to have a commitment from both partners before having sex?” One girl - “For how long?” I would…but only for 6 weeks…after that I’d be afraid there was something wrong with me.”
         1) This is heartbreaking - she would think something’s wrong with her - our society is taught that this is normal
   c) Yet, this is what God’s will/plan/blueprint for us is:
1 Thess 4:3 For this is the will of God, your sanctification: that you should abstain from sexual immorality
      i) Sex is good - inside the moral boundaries of marriage
VS 5 not in passion of lust, like the Gentiles who do not know God;
         1) Passion of lust: means “without object, aim or end; it’s like animals - where a physical urge is only factor
VS 7 For God did not call us to uncleanness, but in holiness
         2) AP says we are called to holiness: our job / vocation: Holiness is our life-long job
      ii) But what is holiness? Holiness is being set-apart - separate from this world - we’re not like them “don’t want to be”
Ill: When someone buys a brand-new white couch, something interesting happens. Nobody sits on it the same way they sit on the old couch. People take their shoes off. They’re careful with drinks. Kids aren’t jumping all over it. Why? Because people protect what they value. But when that same couch gets stained and worn down over the years, people stop caring as much. The difference isn’t the couch — it’s the value people place on it. 
         1) IOW: do you value what God has given you? If see your life /  body /  future as valuable, you’ll protect them
Story: One of the greatest accomplishments of my life is that my wife & I’s first kiss was on our wedding day. The great lie of this world is oh, how can I spend my life with them if I haven’t slept with them? But my wife and I’s marriage is incredible - and I believe it’s so good because we waited - because I valued my relationship with her, not just what I can get physically from her.
   d) Ultimately, our TXT says the motivation for purity is pleasing God
VS 1 Finally then, brethren, we urge and exhort in the Lord Jesus that you should abound more and more, just as you received from us how you ought to walk and to please God
      i) But why does purity please God?
         1) Firstly, because you’re putting God first: above the spirit of age / our own instincts
         2) Secondly, b/c you are honoring / valuing His plan - God’s plan is marriage
Genesis 2:24 Therefore a man shall leave his father and mother and be joined to his wife, and they shall become one flesh.
         3) Thirdly, b/c you are honoring / valuing God’s plans for your life as an individual


2. The Power of Purity
   a) Immorality effects the Spirit of God in the church
Thess 4:4 that each of you should know how to possess his own vessel in sanctification and honor,
      i) Word vessel used a lot in Bible: this is a picture of the tools of the Temple
         1) The Temple was dwelling place for God - literally where man met with God
      ii) The vessels enabled work of God to be accomplished
1 Ki 8:4  And they brought up the ark of the LORD, and the tabernacle of the congregation, and all the holy vessels that were in the tabernacle, even those did the priests and the Levites bring up.
         1) So that’s the old testament; what are the new testament vessels? Our bodies.
1 Cor 6:19  Do you not know that your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit, who is in you, whom you have received from God? You are not your own;you were bought at a price. Therefore honor God with your body.
      iii) SO: Moral impurity in our bodies, affects the Spirit of God
         1) And unclean Christian / unclean church is a powerless Christian / powerless church
Biblical Story: We see in Genesis 38, we read story of Judah who sleeps with a prostitute and she takes his authority: 
Gen 38:16  Not realizing that she was his daughter-in-law, he went over to her by the roadside and said, "Come now, let me sleep with you." "And what will you give me to sleep with you?" she asked. "I'll send you a young goat from my flock," he said. "Will you give me something as a pledge until you send it?" she asked. He said, "What pledge should I give you?" "Your seal and its cord, and the staff in your hand," she answered. So he gave them to her and slept with her, and she became pregnant by him.
            a) He surrendered his dominion / authority (staff / ring) - he lost it b/c of impurity in his body
         2) Ultimate sadness is the devil wants to use God’s vessels for his own purpose - take your power from you
            a) But the enemy presents this as “freedom / sexual revolution” - yet, it’s a bondage
1 Thess 4:7 For God did not call us to uncleanness, but in holiness.
         3) What is uncleanness in TXT? It means pollution / filth - result in OT was they were disqualified from service
            a) So the aim of purity as a Christian is to preserve the Spirit of God in effectiveness
   b) But TXT also shows us that immorality & uncleanness involves THEFT
1 Th 4:6  That no man go beyond and defraud his brother in any matter: because that the Lord is the avenger of all such, as we also have forewarned you and testified.
      i) What does AP mean when says immorality & uncleanness involves theft?
         1) Dating: robbing something from your future marriage
         2) Pornography / fantasy: You’re robbing your spouse / future spouse of effort & energy
         3) Adultery: Robbing your husband / wife / children
         4) In Church: Robbing others around you of presence of God
   c) TXT tells us: the heart that allows lust to dominate is a despising heart
VS 8 TXT 1 Th 4:8  He therefore that despiseth, despiseth not man, but God, who hath also given unto us his holy Spirit.
      i) Despise: means to set aside / make small
         1) This is an attitude that treats immorality lightly - no big deal
Heb 12:16 lest there be any fornicator or profane person like Esau, who for one morsel of food sold his birthright.
         2) Have ever thought: why was it such a big deal that Esau sold his birthright?
Historical Context: In ancient culture, Esau wasn’t just giving away a small privilege. He was giving away: twice the inheritance / leadership of the family / control of the household after the father died. To a modern person, that would be like selling your entire estate, house, business and your family for a bowl of soup.
            a) And Paul says just like Esau despised his birthright, those who are immoral despise things of God
      ii) But thank God there is HOPE: none of us are perfect - we’ve all sinned and fallen short - Bible gives us hope
         1) Hope that we can have supernatural help to remain pure


3. Practical Purity
   a) The key to purity is a heart attitude of honoring God
1 Thess 4:4  That every one of you should know how to possess his vessel in sanctification and honour;
      i) Honor: value / weight / respect - So we honor God, we respect Him
         1) We honor our spouse / honor our church - we value them
         2) We honor our potential: our ability to be used - bring Spirit of God to others
Biblical Story: Joseph understood honor. We see when his master’s wife tries to sleep with him, he tells her this:
Gen 39:9 There is no one greater in this house than I, nor has he kept back anything from me but you, because you are his wife. How then can I do this great wickedness, and sin against God? Then what does Joseph do? Doesn’t stay around talking to her. He runs away.
   b) So let’s talk about some practical steps to purity
      i) Firstly: Bible tells us don’t go where you will be tempted! Block access
Someone said: “Temptation knocks on everyone’s door… but too many people open the door, invite it in, and make it dinner.” - don’t open the door! Don’t let it in
Story: A man once said he was trying to lose weight, but every morning he kept driving past the donut shop on the way to work. One day he came into the office with a whole box of donuts. His coworkers said, “Wait… I thought you were on a diet!” He said, “I am! I prayed this morning and said, ‘Lord, if you want me to have donuts, let there be a parking spot right in front of the shop.’” Then he said, “And sure enough… on the eighth time around the block, a spot opened up.”
Matthew 18:8-9  Wherefore if thy hand or thy foot offend thee, cut them off, and cast them from thee: it is better for thee to enter into life halt or maimed, rather than having two hands or two feet to be cast into everlasting fire. 9 And if thine eye offend thee, pluck it out, and cast it from thee: it is better for thee to enter into life with one eye, rather than having two eyes to be cast into hell fire.
         1) If there are certain apps on phone that tempt you, delete them - certain places you go that tempt you, don’t go
      ii) Ultimately, this is a decision to take control over your body: to rule it - not be ruled by it
1 Cor 9:27  But I keep under my body, and bring it into subjection: lest that by any means, when I have preached to others, I myself should be a castaway.
Ill: When a chef prepares a meal at a restaurant, the chef carefully chooses good ingredients. Why? B/c If you start with rotten ingredients, you can’t cook a good meal. It doesn’t matter how talented the chef is. Bad ingredients = bad outcome. But the same is true spiritually: if we’re constantly feeding ourselves movies, social media, pornography, questionable music, we shouldn’t be surprised when our spiritual life suffers.
Job 31:1  "I made a covenant with my eyes not to look lustfully at a girl.
   c) But the great hope is that our walk w/ God gives us the spiritual strength to be pure
      i) It is natural - it is an instinct to lust, to be immoral
         1) Ultimately it takes supernatural power to do other that what our flesh wants
      ii) How do we activate this supernatural power?
         1) Prayer: Mat 26:41  Watch and pray, that ye enter not into temptation: the spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak.
         2) Word of God: Pastor used to say: The word will keep you from sin, or sin will keep you from the word.
Psa 119:9  How can a young man keep his way pure? By living according to your word.
Psa 119:11  Thy word have I hid in mine heart, that I might not sin against thee.
Closing Ill: I once saw a pastor demonstrate purity with a glass of water. The water was filled with dirt. The water was cloudy and polluted - impure. If you tried to clean it by reaching in with your hand, you would only stir it up more. But if you take that glass and place it under a faucet of clean water and just let the clean water keep flowing, something interesting happens. Slowly the dirty water begins to overflow… and after a while the glass becomes clear again. It becomes pure again. When we begin to fill our lives with purity, our impurities are washed away by the precious blood of Jesus. His purity overflows our impurity and we are left as a pure vessel again.
1 John 1:9 If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness.
- You can not become perfectly pure on your own - you must keep filling yourself with purity of Christ, drawing ever closer to Him, as you draw further and further from impurity.
